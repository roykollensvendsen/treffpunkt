# Score a 10 m air-rifle target

This page explains how Treffpunkt turns the spot you tap into a score.

## The target
A 10 m air-rifle target has ten scoring rings. The bullseye (ring 10) is a tiny
0.5 mm dot; each ring outward is 5 mm wider. A black aiming area covers the
middle of the target.

## Placing a shot
Tap where your shot landed. Treffpunkt places a marker the size of a 4.5 mm
air-rifle pellet and shows the score.

## How the score is decided
- **Whole-ring score (1–10):** like reading a paper target with a gauge — a shot
  counts for a ring if the pellet touches it.
- **Decimal score (up to 10.9):** like an electronic target — the closer to the
  exact centre, the higher the decimal, up to 10.9 dead centre.

The precise rules and numbers are in
[spec 0001](../specs/0001-10m-air-rifle-target-and-scoring.md).
