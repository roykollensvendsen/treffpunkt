# Score a 10 m air-rifle series

This page explains how Treffpunkt records a series of shots and adds up the
score.

## The target
A 10 m air-rifle target has ten scoring rings. The bullseye (ring 10) is a tiny
0.5 mm dot; each ring outward is 5 mm wider. A black aiming area covers the
middle of the target.

## Shooting a series
A *series* is a fixed number of shots on one target face — ten for the 10 m
air-rifle program. Tap where each shot landed; Treffpunkt places a marker the
size of a 4.5 mm pellet and adds the shot to the numbered list, with the running
total updating as you go. The list shows how many shots you have placed (for
example **3 / 10**).

## Adjusting a shot
Need to nudge a shot? **Press and hold** on its marker — it turns blue to show it
is picked up — then **drag** it to the right spot and let go. The score updates
as you move. A quick tap somewhere else simply places the next shot.

## Finishing the series
When every shot is placed, the **✓** in the top bar lights up. Tap it to mark the
series complete; the total card shows it is done and the shots are locked.

## How the score is decided
- **Whole-ring score (1–10):** like reading a paper target with a gauge — a shot
  counts for a ring if the pellet touches it.
- **Series total:** the sum of every shot, shown against the maximum the series
  could reach.

Some disciplines also mark an **inner ten ("X")** — a shot dead in the middle —
used to break ties; the air-rifle target here does not. The precise rules and
numbers are in
[spec 0001](../specs/0001-10m-air-rifle-target-and-scoring.md), with the series
model in [spec 0004](../specs/0004-program-series-and-series-scoring.md).
